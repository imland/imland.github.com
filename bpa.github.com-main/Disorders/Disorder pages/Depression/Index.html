Major Depressive Disorder (Depression)

Depression is a common, treatable medical illness affecting over 21 million U.S. adults (roughly 8.3%), with symptoms ranging from persistent sadness to loss of interest, fatigue, and cognitive difficulties. It is not a sign of weakness, but a complex brain disorder involving genetic, biological, and environmental factors.

Symptoms

Depression symptoms include a persistent low mood, loss of interest in activities, fatigue, changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, and feelings of worthlessness. These symptoms must last at least two weeks to indicate a major depressive disorder. Other signs include irritability, difficulty concentrating, and suicidal thoughts.
