(Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD): Develops after experiencing or witnessing traumatic events.

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a treatable mental health condition caused by experiencing or witnessing terrifying events like abuse, war, or disasters. Symptoms include intrusive memories (flashbacks, nightmares), avoidance of triggers, negative changes in mood/thoughts, and hyperarousal, such as being easily startled. It affects daily life by impairing work, relationships, and functioning.

Symptoms

PTSD symptoms arise after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event, lasting over a month, and interfering with daily life. They are categorized into four main types: reliving the event (flashbacks, nightmares), avoidance of reminders, negative changes in thoughts and mood, and high arousal (anger, jumpiness, insomnia).
