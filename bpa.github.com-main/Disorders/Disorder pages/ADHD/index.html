Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD): Neurodevelopmental disorder marked by persistent inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity.

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a common neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and/or impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning. While often diagnosed in childhood, it frequently persists into adulthood, affecting focus, organization, and emotional regulation. Treatment typically involves a combination of medication, behavioral therapies, and lifestyle support. Inattention: Easily distracted, difficulty sustaining focus, forgetfulness, losing items, disorganization, and trouble following instructions. Hyperactivity: Constant movement, fidgeting, inability to stay seated, and talking excessively. Impulsivity: Acting without thinking, interrupting others, and difficulty waiting for turns.

Symptoms

Inattention Symptoms: Poor Focus: Difficulty sustaining attention in conversations, tasks, or meetings. Disorganization: Struggles with time management, keeping belongings in order, and meeting deadlines. Forgetfulness: Frequently forgetting appointments, chores, or misplaced items (keys, wallet, phone). Careless Mistakes: Difficulty with attention to detail and overlooking tasks. Avoidance: Dislike of tasks requiring sustained mental effort. Hyperactivity & Impulsivity Symptoms: Restlessness: Feeling "revved up," fidgeting, or needing to move constantly. Impulsivity: Acting quickly without thinking, making reckless decisions, or interrupting others. Difficulty Waiting: Trouble waiting in line or waiting for their turn in conversation. Excessive Talking: Talking excessively or blurting out answers.
